SECTIONS 3–4 — DATA

The 5 KPIs that matter most at Red Points

🎯
Risk Score
Composite threat level. Trending up = more exposure. Always show the trend line, not just the number. "Your risk score increased 18% MoM — here's what's driving it."
⚡
Enforcement Rate (ESR)
The headline metric. Below 70% = conversation needed. Above 90% = renewal ammunition. Show trajectory always. Below 50% = urgent escalation.
🌐
Monitoring Scope
Coverage breadth. Gaps = upsell signals. "You're not monitoring TikTok but 18% of your infringements are social. That's an unprotected vector."
🏆
Takedowns
Volume of enforcements. The most tangible win to show clients. "We removed 1,421 infringing listings in Q3." Always tie to economic impact.
💰
Economic Impact
Estimated revenue saved. This is what closes renewals. "We protected an estimated €2.3M in revenue." Use Looker economic model as baseline.
🔄
Detection → Enforcement Funnel
The hidden insight. 10K detections + 100 enforcements = funnel problem, not detection problem. Always analyse conversion at each stage.

SECTIONS 6 & 14 — RISK
Churn Prevention & Risk Intelligence
Detect churn before it happens. Build mitigation plans before it's too late. Protect ARR proactively.

Early warning signals — know them cold

📉
KPI deterioration
ESR drops 10%+ month-over-month. Enforcement volume declining. Risk score rising without corresponding action being taken.
👻
Engagement ghost patterns
Missed QBRs. Unreturned emails for 10+ days. Drop in platform login activity. Shorter meetings. Single-word replies to detailed emails.
🏢
Org structure changes
Champion leaves. New VP joins with different preferences. Budget owner changes. Reorg announcement. Watch LinkedIn for contact updates.
💬
Language signals
"We're evaluating options", "our team is reviewing the contract", "what does the exit process look like?" — direct churn signals. Act immediately.
⚔️
Competitor mentions
Any unprompted mention of a competitor tool. "We've been demoing X" or "another vendor told us..." — go to Crisis + Competitive Intel sections now.
💸
Budget pressure signals
Layoffs announced, "we're optimising vendor spend", industry headwinds. Cross-reference your portfolio against industry news weekly.

SECTION 16 — EBR/QBR
QBR / EBR Master Framework
Build world-class quarterly reviews in minutes. Drive renewals and expansions from every meeting.

The QBR structure that closes renewals

1
Open with the threat landscape — not your product
Start with what's happening in their market: "Counterfeiting in [industry] grew 34% last quarter. Here's how it hit your category and what we caught." This anchors urgency before you show results.
2
Show the before → after transformation (3 numbers only)
Infringements detected. Enforcements executed. Economic impact. Everything else in appendix. Executives remember 3 numbers, not 30 slides.
3
Flag the 2 biggest risks ahead
Never leave a QBR without telling them what's coming. "In Q4, TikTok Shop infringements typically spike 40% for your category. We're preparing X." This is what separates advisors from vendors.
4
One focused expansion recommendation
One module, specific data, clear ROI. Not a menu. A single recommendation with strong justification. Ask for the decision in the meeting — not "think about it."
5
Close with mutual commitments
Both sides commit. "We will deliver X by [date]. You will connect us with [stakeholder] by [date]." End by sending the next QBR calendar invite before you hang up.

SECTION 18 — NAVIGATION
Stakeholder Navigation
Map influence, build champions, neutralise blockers. The political game is part of the job.
🗺️
Power map first
Before any strategic move, map who has power, who has interest, who influences the decision-maker. The org chart lies — find the real power structure.
🏆
Invest in your champion
Champions are built, not found. Give them wins, data they can present internally, and language that makes them look good to their boss. Their success = your renewal.
🚧
Neutralise blockers strategically
Don't fight blockers — understand their concern and reframe. A legal VP blocking an upsell is protecting their company. Show them RP protects their interests too.
📡
Multi-thread always
Single-threaded accounts die when that contact leaves. Build relationships at 3 levels: user, manager, executive. LinkedIn connections + internal intro requests.
🎭
Different message per persona
Legal: compliance. Brand: reputation. CFO: avoided losses. Ecom: operational efficiency. Same data, translated to each person's world. Always.
⚡
New exec = 48-hour window
When a new exec joins: reach out within 48 hours, send a "here's what we've done for your team" summary, request a call. Speed wins goodwill.

MASTER PLAYBOOKS
CSM Playbooks
Step-by-step guides for the highest-stakes CSM scenarios. Follow the sequence.
🔴The 90-Day Renewal Playbook
▼
D-90
Internal prep
Run KPI health check. Map all stakeholders. Identify top 3 value gaps and risks. Prepare "value story" with economic impact numbers. Flag to manager if ARR is above threshold. Use prompts #01, #05, #07.
D-75
Proactive outreach to champion
Email champion with QBR invite + preliminary value summary. Subject: "Preparing your Q[X] brand protection review — early preview." Do not mention renewal yet. Send using prompt #11.
D-60
QBR / EBR delivery
Run strategic review using the QBR framework. Lead with threat landscape, show economic impact, present one expansion recommendation. Confirm renewal intent verbally: "We're planning to continue — can we align on the paperwork?" Use prompts #27–#30.
D-45
Formal renewal proposal
Send value justification pack. Include: YoY comparison, economic impact, RP roadmap for next year, and proposed terms. Ask for decision by D-30. Use prompts #12 + #19.
D-30
Decision follow-up
If no response: phone call only, not email. Handle objections with battlecard (prompt #41). Escalate internally if ARR at risk — loop in manager or AE. Do not drop the urgency.
D-0
Close + plan next cycle
Confirm signature. Send thank-you + Q1 plan preview. Immediately send calendar invite for next QBR. Log all learnings in CRM. Start next 90-day cycle.
🚀The Upsell Motion Playbook
▼
1️⃣
Find the gap in detection data first
Look where infringements appear in data that RP doesn't currently cover. TikTok infringements without social module = perfect setup. Quantify the exposure before saying anything.
2️⃣
Quantify the exposure — show the number
"We detected 847 TikTok infringements last quarter that we couldn't enforce because it's outside scope." Make the gap visible and painful before offering the solution.
3️⃣
One recommendation, not a menu
Never present 3 expansion options. One prioritised recommendation + clear justification. Choice paralyses. Recommendation accelerates. Use prompt #22.
4️⃣
Ask for the meeting, not the close
CTA in proposal email: "Can we get 30 minutes to walk through the data?" — not "can you approve this?" Lower friction = higher conversion rate. Always.
5️⃣
Use QBR as the expansion moment
The expansion conversation closes faster when the client already feels the value. QBR = perfect moment. Show wins first, then the gap. Then the solution.
🛡️The Churn Rescue Playbook
▼
⚠️
Trigger this playbook when: Account missed 2+ meetings, KPIs declining 2+ months, negative sentiment expressed, or direct churn signal received.
🔍
Diagnose before you act
Run prompt #17 (Risk Scan) + prompt #20 (Rescue Plan). The actual problem is rarely what it looks like on the surface. Mis-diagnose = wrong solution.
📞
Phone call only — stop emailing
When a client is at churn risk, stop sending emails. Call directly. Email feels low-effort and gets ignored. A phone call signals you care and creates urgency.
🏢
Escalate to RP leadership if ARR > threshold
Don't fight this alone. An executive-to-executive call from RP leadership to their leadership is often the pattern-interrupt needed to reset the relationship.
🎁
Give a win before asking for renewal
Before renewing the contract conversation, deliver something concrete: a custom analysis, a quick enforcement win, a feature they'd been waiting for. Give first. Renew second.

One ask per executive meeting
Every executive meeting ends with one specific ask: a decision, an introduction, a commitment. More than one ask = zero asks get completed. Decide your ask before the meeting starts.
📊
Lead with their business goal — not your product
Open: "You mentioned [goal X] was a priority last time — here's how Q[X] tracked against that." This shows you remembered, connected dots, and are thinking like a partner, not a vendor.
⏰
Leave 10 minutes for their questions
Never run out of time before the Q&A. Executives reveal their real concerns in questions. That's your intelligence for the next engagement and your upsell signal.

Prompt engineering for CSMs

🎭
Always define: role + audience + constraint
The best prompts define who you are (RP CSM), who reads the output (VP Legal / CFO / Brand Director), and the constraint (max 150 words / no jargon / executive tone). Without all three, AI gives generic output.
❌ "Write an email about the KPI results" ✅ "Write a 130-word email to a VP of Legal at a luxury brand. Highlight 2 enforcement wins. Quantify economic impact. No technical jargon. Close with one specific ask."
📊
Paste raw data — don't describe it
Instead of "last quarter we had 3,200 enforcements" — paste the actual Looker table. AI extracts patterns from raw data far better than from your description of it. CSV rows, copy-pasted Google Sheet tables, Gong transcript text all work.
✂️
The "sharpen" command
After any output type: "Sharpen this — cut 30%, make it more direct, and add a stronger opening line." This single command turns a good draft into a great one. Works on emails, QBR narratives, and executive summaries every time.
🎯
Ask for 3 versions, pick the best
For sensitive emails or renewal negotiations: "Give me 3 versions with different tones: assertive, collaborative, and empathetic." Then pick or blend. This is faster than editing one draft repeatedly.
🧠
Use AI as a thinking partner, not just a writer
Before a hard conversation: "I need to tell [client] their ESR dropped 15% but it's not our fault. Walk me through how to frame this to preserve the relationship and turn it into an upsell trigger." Strategic coaching on demand.
⚡
The executive translation workaround
Take any technical RP explanation and run: "Translate this for a CFO who has never heard of Red Points. One paragraph. Focus only on financial impact." Use for economic impact model, enforcement methodology, or detection algorithm explanations.
🔍
Competitive research in 2 minutes with Perplexity
In Perplexity.ai: "What are the top complaints about [competitor] on G2 and Capterra in 2025? What do their customers say they wish was better?" Real ammunition for competitive conversations, sourced from verified reviews.
📋
Build your personal prompt library
Keep a Google Doc called "My RP Prompt Library" with 10-15 prompts pre-filled with your typical context (your portfolio size, common client types, etc.). Update the variable parts only. Cuts prep time from 20 minutes to 90 seconds.

The 3 biggest AI mistakes CSMs make

❌
Sending AI output without reviewing it. AI doesn't know your client's relationship history, political context, or last week's conversation. Always review for accuracy, tone, and personal relevance before sending. You add the judgment.
⚠️
Using AI numbers without verifying. AI hallucinates. If it generates economic impact calculations or benchmark comparisons, verify against Looker data. Use AI for framing, structure, and language — use RP data for the actual numbers.
✅
The 80/20 rule: AI handles 80% of drafting, structure, and pattern recognition. You bring 20% — the client relationship context, the strategic judgment, the final editing. That 20% is your irreplaceable value.

The RP CSM golden rules

🥇
Data first, opinion second
Every recommendation must be grounded in RP data. "Our data shows..." always beats "I think..." in executive conversations.
📅
Proactive beats reactive
If you're responding to a client problem, you're already behind. Weekly portfolio scans prevent crises. Run prompt #05 every Monday.
🎯
One metric, one story
Pick the one KPI that tells the most powerful story in each conversation. Don't overwhelm with dashboards. Simplicity persuades.
💰
Always translate to money
Enforcement rates don't close renewals. "We protected €2.3M in revenue last year" does. Always convert metrics to economic impact.
🤝
Relationship > transaction
The best renewals happen because the client trusts their CSM. That trust is built in the small moments — not just at QBR time.
🔄
Iterate and compound
Save what works, discard what doesn't. Build your personal prompt library. Your RP playbook compounds over time into an unfair advantage.
